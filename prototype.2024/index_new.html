The Magic City Beats

Genre: 2D Action Platformer

Engine: GameMaker 2

Role(s): Creator

Development: Early 2023 - Current

I developed the framework for my senior capstone “The Magic City Beats,” a game concept heavily inspired by the Castlevania series, but with a stronger focus on platforming, tight controls, and minimalist yet stylish combat mechanics. My intention for this project was to not just create a game that players could only wish was fully complete, but to also be the best project presented at my senior capstone, and undisputably so. This project was created using Game Maker Studio 2 alongside Microsoft Paint for the development and the sprite work respectively. And this demo version of my capstone acts as the progenitor, benchmark, and guideline for the rest of development moving forward. Ultimately, Magic City Beats will display all of what I’ve learned throughout my academic career; game design, storytelling, art, and music, while also doubling as a love letter to my hometown, Gary, IN. In short, I expect it to be my magnum opus.

Supermoon 64

Genre: Rail Shooter

Engine: Unity

Role(s): Co-Creator

Development: October 2023 - December 2023

I co-developed an on-rails shooter by the name of “Supermoon 64,” which took the player through a world of over-the-top concepts and nonsensical humor as a way to both have a laugh at, and have a laugh with, the tropes this project pays homage to. Such tropes involve concepts popularized in Japanese media as well as the American appropriation of said tropes via ‘Mary Sues.’ I was responsible for the user interface of this project, as well as the sound design, player models, and game-narrative. The project was created in Unity, but the 3D models were made in Blender and the majority of the UI and heads up display, or HUD, was made in Microsoft Paint. This project was my first introduction to the practical reality of game development within a group and sharing responsibilities for the overall quality of a project. And ultimately, this project expresses my skills to thoroughly have enjoyment in building around such a concept as ‘joy’ and ‘passion’ are what retains a game’s integrity throughout its development.

Design Documents and Worldbuilding

Type: Pre-Production and Project Management

Role(s): Narrative Game Development

I created a story-focused video essay for the indie game, Salt and Sanctuary, titled “Journey in a Bottle”. This project consisted of recording a considerably sizeable amount of footage before syphoning through 200 GBs worth of recordings and then organizing it in a cohesive and coherent end-product. The video essay was made by using OBS Studios for the recording aspect and Vegas Pro 18 for the editing. While Journey in a Bottle was a personal project, it had proved my practical skills of being able to thoroughly comprehend the entirety of a game’s world and narrative, and then be able to express said world and narrative toward a large group of people. This is a particularly useful skill as a designer who focuses on game mechanics and narrative through game mechanics, as well as an aspiring project lead who needs to convey the tone and story of a project to their team.

Art Gallery

Type: Digital Art

Software: Blender, Maya, Adobe Photoshop, Aseprite, MS Paint

I designed and programmed a gallery of all my prominent artwork, ranging from 2D Illustrations to Pixel Animations and 3D Animations. As an undergraduate of Media Arts and Science with a specialization in game design and development, I thought it would be beneficial to have a gallery of my skills outside of my main focus to quickly express said skills in an unintrusive manner and be displayed without cluttering the rest of my portfolio. The art displayed ranges from multiple projects as well as left over concept ideas that would not have seen the light of day otherwise. As with my portfolio itself, the gallery is displayed in order by relevance and category using HTML 5 programming, but the software used for the artwork ranges from MS Paint, Aseprite, Photoshop, Maya, Blender, and FL Studio. To reiterate, this gallery is a concise showcase of my secondary skills as a Bachelor of Science.
